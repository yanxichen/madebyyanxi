“

Eyes of mnemosyne – follow me down to that earliest midsummer, mid-afternoon stroll under the tree-filtered sunlight of north-western China; and return to my room that was tinted blue by evenings, a room that started to look like many other rooms as I grew; and find me in it, first with feet propped up on a step stool, then with them on the floor – concrete, hardwood, carpet – always trying to count the lines between little black dots with my right index finger – inexplicable languages that hid ancient, secret knowledge, which, with some luck and much labour, will transform into great melodies through flutterings of tessellated black and ivory. The piano, where I'll always return, if only to find its dark veneer still glossy, the keys still icy to touch, and the notes still at familiar frequencies, and I will know that everything is where it should be...

"
